Human nutrition

Over 40 different substances are indispensable in human nutrition and must be obtained from food.
They include 10 amino acids, 13 vitamins, 20 or more mineral elements, and one or more polyunsaturated fatty acids.

To lose 25 lb (11.3 kg) of body fat, one needs to consume 300 fewer calories per day for 83 weeks,
or 600 fewer calories per day for 42 weeks, or 1200 fewer per day for 21 weeks.

The nutritional value or quality of a given protein depends upon two factors: 1. its content of the essential amino acids and 2. its digestibility.

Although plant proteins are in general nutritionally inferior to those from animal sources, certain combinations of plant proteins can provide a completely adequate and balanced mixture of essential amino acids.

Conclusion

There is no single perfect food that provides all nutritional needs for everyone.
The 40 different required nutrients occur in very different proportions in different foods.

Daily intake of required nutrients: variety within each food group is essential.

The choices from each group should be varied from day to day,
to avoid consumption of one or a few foods to the exclusion of most others in that group.

The best guarantee of adequate nutrition is a highly varied diet,
together with an appropriate balance of calories and proteins in relation to individual needs.
